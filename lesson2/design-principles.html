Design Principles

Visual Hierarchy

Mejuri

mejuri.com

I feel as if this page properly uses Visual Hierarchy due to the visual cleanliness of the site and the arrangement of each of the items on it. It organized things in order of importance, having all of the important links to navigate the site up at the top, with appropriate images to pull you in just below, ordered neatly left to right, top to bottom, with proper sizing. It follows exactly where the user's eyes want to trace. It is properly split up into sections such as every day jewelry, valentines day gifts, etc.

Rule of Thirds

West Elm

westelm.com

I threw this one under rule of thirds due to their use of spacing, dividing the first third of the website to the important words and details that they would like you to read and notice, and the other two thirds for supporting images and things of the like. If you scroll down to the second set of images, you'll see a portion that says, "New year, clean slate..." I feel as if this is a perfect example of how to split the space of your website properly into thirds.

White Space

Urban Outfitters

urbanoutfitters.com

Urban Outfitters properly uses white, negative spacing in order to differentiate and divide between images, links, typography, and products. This allows for the eyes to easily jump from object to object, without any fear of missing something in a blur of different objects on the page. In fact, nothing passes the eyes unseen.
